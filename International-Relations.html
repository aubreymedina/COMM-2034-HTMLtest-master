    Class Overview

    This course is co-taught by Dr. Stivachtis and Professor Donahue. This course explores the relationship between international law and international governance and is collaboratively tought by Dr. Stivachtis and Professor Connor. Examination of the scope, sources and politics of international law. There is discussion about the structure, function, and policies of major global and regional organizations, such as the United Nations (UN), The World Trade Organization (WTO), the IMF/World Bank Group, NATO, the European Union (EU), the Organization for Security & Cooperation in Europe (OSCE), the Shanghai Cooperation Organization (SCO), the ASEAN, the African Union (AU), the Organization of American States (OAS) and MERCOSUR. We also investigate policies and politics major interstate groups, such as the G-7/8, the G-20, and the BRICS.


    Specifically, you will learn the following:

  • The scope and sources of international law, as well as the politics pertaining to its formulation and implementation
  • The structure, function, and policies of major global and regional organizations
  • The policies and politics of major interstate groups

    • My Take

    I enjoy this class because I hope to work for an organization similar to the ones we study in class. The structure of the class is pretty easy to follow: lecture, and then exam on the lecture. It is clear which lectures will be on the exams; thus, it is rather easy to study for them. The PowerPoints are posted on Canvas, but it is more beneficial to go to class because the slides do not include all the important details that may be on the exams. The exams are straightforward: there are 10 essay questions, but you are to pick two to answer. The lectures are easier to follow and more intriguing when Professor Donahue teaches them; he's very straightforward and clear, whereas Dr. Stivachtis has a thick Greek accent and often rambles, making it somewhat hard to understand him. Overall, I enjoy this class! If only I was a morning person and could make it all the time (it's at 9:30 a.m.)
